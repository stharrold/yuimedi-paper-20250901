# News & Perspectives Article Guidelines

**Source:** https://support.jmir.org/hc/en-us/articles/41013797143963-News-Perspectives-Article-Guidelines

---

The News and Perspectives section of the Journal of Medical Internet Research features grounded, well-researched news stories and original perspectives in digital health. News and Perspectives articles are different from research manuscripts and undergo separate submission and review processes. They are commissioned or invited news-type articles.

## Editorial Vision

### Scope

News and Perspectives articles include timely, insightful analyses, interviews, and commentary that go deeper than traditional news headlines and reflect a diverse range of voices. Topics include:

- Artificial intelligence and health data
- Digital therapeutics and consumer health
- Science policy, regulation, and publishing trends
- Research findings and clinical applications
- Emerging technologies
- Privacy, security, and ethics
- Business and entrepreneurship
- Health equity and access

### Aims

News and Perspectives articles keep researchers, industry experts, policymakers, healthcare professionals, and innovators informed and inspired. They provide credible, trustworthy information that helps:

- Stimulate new research ideas and tech developments
- Inform policy decisions
- Facilitate ethical, effective practice

### Values

- **Rigor:** Thoroughness and accuracy in research and reporting
- **Integrity:** Honesty, unbiased, balanced, transparent, fair, and conflict of interest-free
- **Intellectual responsibility:** Thoughtfulness, diverse perspectives, no oversimplification or misrepresentation
- **Empowerment:** Actionable and meaningful, provides knowledge or tools for informed decisions

## Eligible Authors and Content

### Author Eligibility

Authors need:
- Deep expertise, experience, and/or passion for digital health
- A keen eye for patterns, trends, or meaningful events
- The ability to write engaging, incisive, high-quality articles

Authors may be journalists, scientific writers, researchers, thought leaders, entrepreneurs, or other digital health experts.

### Types of Content

- **Analysis/Commentary:** In-depth examination of a specific trend, policy, research finding, or controversy
- **Op-Eds/Opinion Pieces:** Well-reasoned, evidence-based statements on an issue
- **News Analysis:** Deeper analysis of current news, going beyond headlines
- **"How-To" or "Lessons Learned" Pieces:** Practical advice or insights from real-world experience
- **Interviews with Key Thought Leaders:** Q&A pieces with influential researchers, policymakers, business leaders

## Commissioning Process

Articles are commissioned through:
- Specific commission requests via social media and website call-outs
- Open pitch calls or calls for collaboration

All articles are compensated at **1.35 CAD per word**.

## Pitch Guidelines

Pitches should include:
- A compelling title
- Key insights or information (3-5 bullet points)
- Rationale for relevance, value, and timeliness (1-3 sentences)
- Planned sources (3-5 bullet points)
- Proposed word count (typical range: 800-1200 words)
- Brief bio with name, role, 2-3 previous publication links, and relevant expertise

## Article Instructions

### Submission Details

- **File format:** Google Doc or Word Document
- **File name:** "N&P_Your last name_Abbreviated article title_ddmmyy"
- **Article length:** Typically 800-1200 words

### Images

- Provide up to three images with short captions (<10 words)
- Use freely available, CC-BY licensed, or permission-granted images
- High-quality PNG files, maximum 5MB and 1200x1200px

### Required Components

1. **Title:** Brief, descriptive, engaging but not overly provocative
2. **Keywords:** At least 5 keywords for indexing
3. **Key Takeaways:** 2-3 bullet points of core messages
4. **References:** All claims supported and sources cited
5. **Conflict of Interest Statement:** Include even if "None declared"

### Use of GenAI

- May be used for process-oriented tasks (literature searches, summarizing, formatting)
- May NOT be used to draft or write articles in whole or in part

## Review and Publication Process

Articles are not peer-reviewed but undergo scientific review. Evaluation criteria:

- **Accuracy:** Claims supported by evidence, appropriately contextualized
- **Credibility:** Sources credible, appropriate, properly cited
- **Objectivity:** Language measured, not sensationalized, positions substantiated
- **Clarity:** Scientific language suitable for audience
- **Balance:** Multiple perspectives acknowledged for controversial topics
- **Transparency:** Conflicts of interest disclosed

After review, articles proceed to copy and production editing.
