## 4. Narration Accuracy -- For the Record

The following narration issues are noted for the record. I understand that changes to the video itself may require a new production cycle, so I am documenting these primarily for future reference and in case a re-edit becomes feasible.

### 4a. Prescriptive Framing of a Descriptive Framework

**Current narration**: "The researchers suggest AI translation of natural language to SQL queries for clinicians" and "The researchers suggest that clinicians need accessible data tools that do not increase workloads."

**Problem**: The manuscript explicitly presents a **descriptive** framework (one that reveals interconnections between challenges), not a **prescriptive** one (one that recommends solutions). The Validated Query Cycle is positioned as an "illustrative application" of how the framework might inform technology design, not as a recommendation. The word "suggest" implies the paper endorses NL2SQL as a solution, which could conflict with the paper's stated scope during peer review.

**Preferred language (if re-edit is possible)**: Replace "the researchers suggest" with "as an illustration, the researchers describe" or "the framework points to."

### 4b. Unsupported Cross-Industry Claim

**Current narration**: "Healthcare lags behind other fields in data analysis for generating insights and improving outcomes."

**Problem**: The paper does not make this cross-industry comparison. The paper documents healthcare's low analytics maturity in absolute terms (HIMSS AMAM Stages 0-3) but does not benchmark it against other industries.

### 4c. Missing Key Statistics

The original author brief provided specific statistics (39 organizations at HIMSS AMAM Stage 6-7, 53% of CIOs with tenure under three years, 79% reporting digital health shortages, 63% increase in self-service adoption). None appear in the final video. While I understand the format constraints, even one or two headline numbers would significantly strengthen the video's credibility for an academic audience.
