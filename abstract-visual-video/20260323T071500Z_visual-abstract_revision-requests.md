## 1. Left Panel ("Interacting organizational and technical challenges...") -- Causal Direction

**Priority: High**

**Current layout**: The left panel shows a linear top-to-bottom cascade:

> Low analytics maturity --> High leadership turnover --> Skill shortage --> Institutional memory loss

**Problem**: The manuscript defines a specific causal chain (Introduction, Section 1): Analytics Maturity is the **observable symptom** (Pillar 1), Workforce Instability is the **active driver** (Pillar 2), and Technical Barriers are the **root mechanism** (Pillar 3). The current cascade implies that low maturity *causes* turnover, but the paper argues the reverse: turnover drives low maturity, and technical barriers perpetuate the cycle. The paper's core insight is that these challenges are **cyclical and self-reinforcing**, not a linear chain.

**Requested change**: Revise the left panel to show a cyclical or circular relationship among the three challenges rather than a one-directional cascade. If a cycle is not feasible within the current layout, reversing the arrow direction (institutional memory loss --> low analytics maturity, i.e., bottom-to-top) and adding a return arrow from the hospital icon back to the top would convey the compounding loop.

## 2. Right Panel ("The Compounding Crisis") -- Replace with the Validated Query Cycle

**Priority: High**

**Current state**: The right panel presents three qualitative outcomes under a "Compounding Crisis" header. This content overlaps with the left panel (which already conveys the problem) and omits the paper's most distinctive contribution.

**Problem**: The manuscript's third contribution (Section 1.5, Figures 1-2) is the **Validated Query Cycle**, a six-step process (Query --> Generation --> Validation --> Storage --> Retrieval --> Persistence) that converts tacit knowledge into durable organizational memory. This is the paper's most actionable and differentiating element -- the mechanism that breaks the compounding crisis. Without it, the graphic shows only "here are three problems" without "here is a mechanism to address them."

The current panel also contains a critical text error: "NL2SQL improving but, sufficiently inaccurate" reverses the paper's finding that models are "not yet sufficiently **accurate** for unsupervised use" (Abstract, Results; Section 3.3, citing Ziletti & D'Ambrosi 2024).

**Requested change**: Replace the "Compounding Crisis" right panel with a simplified representation of the Validated Query Cycle. This resolves both the missing content and the text error.

A minimal version could show the six steps as icons in a circle with a label such as "Validated Query Cycle: captures expertise before it is lost." The paper's Figure 2 (attached) can serve as a reference for the cycle's structure.

The new three-panel flow would read: **Problem** (left: compounding challenges) --> **Framework** (center: three pillars) --> **Mechanism** (right: Validated Query Cycle). This gives the viewer a complete narrative arc.
