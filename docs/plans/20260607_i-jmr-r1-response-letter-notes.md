## IMRD-vs-Viewpoint PUSHBACK (key response-letter point)
The editor's instruction that "all subheadings must be under Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion" reflects the IMRD structure for Original Papers and does not apply to this article type. This manuscript is a **Viewpoint**. Per JMIR's Instructions for Authors / article-type definitions:
- Viewpoint manuscript structure is **unstructured**, and "using descriptive section headers that organize the work according to the content presented is strongly recommended."
- "Headings such as 'Methods' and 'Results' are **not allowed** in Viewpoints."

(Source: JMIR article-types guidance, mirrored in `standards/jmir_submission_article-types.md`.)

Therefore the manuscript intentionally uses descriptive section headers rather than IMRD headings, consistent with the Viewpoint format. We have addressed the other formatting items (no numbered headings; no single-subheading sections; italics no longer used in place of subheadings), and respectfully note that IMRD subheadings would conflict with the Viewpoint article type. Suggested polite phrasing for the letter: confirm compliance with the applicable items and cite the Viewpoint structure rule for the IMRD item, deferring to the editor if they nonetheless require restructuring (which would entail re-designating the article type).
