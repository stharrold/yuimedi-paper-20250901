# Healthcare IT Employee Training Time - Research Findings

## Summary

The average time to train a healthcare IT employee varies significantly depending on the role and training type. Research suggests ranges from approximately 1 month to 3 years:

| Training Type | Duration |
|---------------|----------|
| Basic EMR/EHR Training for End-Users | 8 hours to 2 months |
| Health Information Workforce Development | 18 months to 2 years |
| IT Developer/Specialist Onboarding | Up to 3 years |
| Formal Health Informatics Specialists | 1 year minimum (60 ECTS credits) |

## Detailed Findings

### 1. Basic EMR/EHR Training for End-Users

- **8 hours (~1 day)**: EMR eLearning training for newly hired inpatient nurses
  - Smailes, P.S., Zurmehly, J., & Schubert, C. (2019). An electronic medical record training conversion for onboarding inpatient nurses. *CIN: Computers, Informatics, Nursing*. journals.lww.com

- **1 month**: Paramedics became proficient with new EMR systems after the first month
  - McLeod, A., Clark, J., & Warren, J. (2008). The impact of information systems on end user performance: Examining the effects of cognitive style using learning curves in an electronic medical record. *Communications of the Association for Information Systems*. papers.ssrn.com

- **2 months (3-4 days/week)**: Extended ICT training for 600 healthcare professionals
  - Rhodes, B., Short, A., & Shaben, T. (2017). Effectiveness of training strategies that support informatics competency development in healthcare professionals. *Health Professionals' Education in the Age of Clinical Information Systems*. Elsevier.

### 2. Health Information Workforce Development

- **18 months**: Five training sessions over this period for new M&E Officers
- **2 years**: Full development of a new health information cadre in Botswana
  - Ledikwe, J.H., Reason, L.L., Burnett, S.M., & Busang, L. (2013). Establishing a health information workforce: innovation for low-and middle-income countries. *Human Resources for Health*. Springer. https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/1478-4491-11-35

### 3. IT Developer/Specialist Onboarding

- **Up to 3 years**: For new developers to become fully fluent in a project
  - Konrad, I., & Saidizand, A. (2022). Exploring the Potential of an IT Capability in its Bootstrap Phase from a Task Driven Onboarding Perspective: Insights toward Information Infrastructure in Healthcare. diva-portal.org

### 4. Formal Biomedical/Health Informatics Specialists

- **1 year minimum (60 ECTS credits)**: Recommended for specialists in BMHI
  - Mantas, J., Ammenwerth, E., & Demiris, G. (2010). Recommendations of the International Medical Informatics Association (IMIA) on education in biomedical and health informatics. *Methods of Information in Medicine*. thieme-connect.com (Cited by: 570)

### 5. Additional Relevant Research

- **6-month EHR training program**: Personalized training for wellness staff members
  - Musa, S., Dergaa, I., & Al Shekh Yasin, R. (2023). The impact of training on electronic health records related knowledge, practical competencies, and staff satisfaction. *Journal of Multidisciplinary Healthcare*. Taylor & Francis.

- **3.5 hours formal training (with 75 min for digital systems)**: Hospital induction for junior doctors, though daily ward-based experience was more effective
  - Benwell, N., Hird, K., Thomas, N., & Furness, E. (2017). Effectiveness and efficiency of training in digital healthcare packages: training doctors to use digital medical record keeping software. *Australian Health Review*. connectsci.au

- **Training should be distributed over time** (not one-time event)
  - Brødsjø, V., Sandøy, B., & Hustad, E. (2023). Exploring onboarding processes for IT professionals: The role of knowledge management. *European Conference on Knowledge Management*. books.google.com

## Key Insights

1. Daily hands-on experience is often more effective than formal training hours
2. Training should be delivered close in time to actual system use
3. Higher levels of training correlate with better perception of HIT benefits
4. On-the-job training and mentoring can substitute for long-term off-site training
5. The onboarding process should be tailored and distributed over time
