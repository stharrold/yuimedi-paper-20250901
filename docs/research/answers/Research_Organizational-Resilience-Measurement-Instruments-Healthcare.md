# Research: Organizational Resilience Measurement Instruments in Healthcare

**Question:** What validated measurement instruments or scales exist for assessing organizational resilience in healthcare settings, and how have they been psychometrically evaluated for construct validity and inter-rater reliability?

**Scope:** Paper1 (supports R5: ARI positioning)

**Date searched:** 2026-03-28

**Source:** Google Scholar Labs (evaluated 61 top results, surfaced 10 relevant papers)

---

## Key Findings

1. **No consensus instrument exists** for measuring organizational resilience in healthcare (Ignatowicz et al. 2023).
2. Only **4 measures** have been developed specifically for healthcare settings, and only **2 have been validated** (Ratliff et al. 2025).
3. **23 distinct instruments** have been applied in health facility contexts, but most lack comprehensive psychometric evaluation (Pasha et al. 2025).
4. The newest instrument (RHCA, Ellis et al. 2026) explicitly correlates resilience with **turnover intention** and patient safety outcomes, directly relevant to the paper's argument.
5. None of the existing instruments address **analytics capability resilience** specifically, supporting the ARI as filling a gap.

## Relevance to Paper

- **R5 (ARI enhancement):** The ARI can be positioned within this established but fragmented measurement landscape. Cite Ignatowicz 2023 for "lack of consensus" and Pasha 2025 for the inventory of 23 instruments to show the ARI extends existing work into analytics-specific resilience.
- **R5 (psychometric caveat):** The finding that only 2 of 4 healthcare-specific tools are validated reinforces the need for psychometric development of the ARI before deployment.
- **Ellis et al. 2026** is especially relevant because the RHCA correlates resilience with turnover intention, directly paralleling the ARI's focus on workforce churn.

## Sources

### 1. Pasha A, Morris K, Nayem J, Kaczmarski K, Li X, et al. Measurement Instruments Assessing Organizational Resilience in Health Facilities: A Systematic Review of Psychometric Properties. medRxiv. 2025.

- **URL:** https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.06.11.25329057.abstract
- **PDF:** https://www.medrxiv.org/content/medrxiv/early/2025/06/11/2025.06.11.25329057.full.pdf
- **Cited by:** 3
- **Key findings:**
  - Identifies 23 distinct organizational resilience instruments applied in health facilities
  - Construct validity was evaluated in 24 of the included studies
  - Nurse Team Resilience Scale was the most psychometrically robust tool among those reviewed

### 2. Chuang S, Ou JC, Hollnagel E, Hou SK. Measurement of resilience potential - development of a resilience assessment grid for emergency departments. PLoS ONE. 2020.

- **URL:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0239472
- **PDF:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article/file?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0239472&type=printable
- **Cited by:** 41
- **Key findings:**
  - Develops the Emergency Department-Resilience Assessment Grid (ED-RAG) from Hollnagel's original RAG
  - 38 questions across 32 foci covering potentials to respond, monitor, anticipate, and learn
  - Inter-rater reliability: Fleiss' Kappa = 0.93
  - Content validity: Windle's score = 1.98

### 3. Ratliff HC, Lee KA, Buchbinder M, Kelly LA, et al. Organizational resilience in healthcare: a scoping review. Journal of Healthcare Management. 2025;70(3).

- **URL:** https://journals.lww.com/jhmonline/fulltext/2025/05000/organizational_resilience_in_healthcare__a_scoping.4.aspx
- **Cited by:** 8
- **Key findings:**
  - Only 4 measures of organizational resilience developed for healthcare settings
  - Only 2 of those 4 have been validated
  - Highlights Benchmark Resilience Tool (BRT) in 53-item (BRT-53) and 13-item (BRT-13) versions
  - Specifically described reliability and validity of instruments

### 4. Ellis LA, Lyng HB, Haraldseid-Driftland C, Saba M, et al. From theory to measurement: Development and initial validation of the Resilience in Healthcare Capacities Assessment (RHCA). Applied Ergonomics. 2026.

- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0003687026000463
- **Cited by:** new (2026)
- **Key findings:**
  - 33-item, ten-factor structure for measuring resilience in healthcare organizations
  - Psychometric evaluation via content analysis, exploratory and confirmatory factor analysis (CFA)
  - High internal consistency across factors
  - **Criterion validity:** significant correlations with staff well-being (Emotional Thriving, Burnout, Turnover Intention) and patient safety outcomes (Safety Climate, Patient Safety Grade)

### 5. Norouzinia R, Yarmohammadian MH, Ferdosi M, et al. Development and psychometric evaluation of the emergency nurses' professional resilience tool. PLoS ONE. 2022.

- **URL:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0269539
- **PDF:** https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article/file?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0269539&type=printable
- **Cited by:** 17
- **Key findings:**
  - Five factors: professional competencies, emotional-cognitive characteristics, external support, behavioral and cognitive strategies
  - Construct validity via exploratory and confirmatory factor analysis
  - Cronbach's alpha: 0.915
  - Intraclass correlation: 0.888

### 6. Ignatowicz A, Tarrant C, Mannion R, El-Sawy D, et al. Organizational resilience in healthcare: a review and descriptive narrative synthesis of approaches to resilience measurement and assessment in empirical studies. BMC Health Services Research. 2023.

- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12913-023-09242-9
- **PDF:** https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1186/s12913-023-09242-9.pdf
- **Cited by:** 68
- **Key findings:**
  - Reviews 35 empirical studies measuring organizational resilience in healthcare
  - **Lack of consensus** on how to evaluate organizational resilience, what should be measured, and which indicators to use
  - Classifies approaches into 5 thematic areas: type of shock, stage of resilience, characteristics/indicators, nature of output, purpose

### 7. Dogrusoz LA, Omay EGG. Organizational resilience in healthcare organizations: A research in the public and private sector. Afet ve Risk Dergisi. 2022.

- **URL:** https://dergipark.org.tr/en/pub/afet/article/1083886
- **PDF:** https://dergipark.org.tr/en/download/article-file/2294201
- **Cited by:** 13
- **Key findings:**
  - Uses BRT-13B (short version of Benchmark Resilience Tool)
  - Confirms original two-factor structure via factor analysis
  - Compares public vs private hospital resilience; differentiation in 6 indicators of planning and adaptive capacity

### 8. Mao X, Chen K, Hu X, Wen X, Loke AY. Establishment of the psychometric properties of a disaster resilience measuring tool for healthcare rescuers in China: a cross-sectional study. International Journal of Disaster Risk Reduction. 2021.

- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13753-021-00342-w
- **PDF:** https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s13753-021-00342-w.pdf
- **Cited by:** 10
- **Key findings:**
  - Disaster Resilience Measuring Tool (DRMT) for healthcare rescue workers
  - Four-factor model: self-efficacy, social support, positive growth, altruism
  - Construct validity via EFA and CFA
  - ICC > 0.85, Cronbach's alpha > 0.84

### 9. Nicolay CR, Williams SP, Brkic M, et al. Measuring the organisational health of acute sector healthcare organisations: Development and validation of the Healthcare-OH survey. International Journal of Healthcare Management. 2021.

- **URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/shareview/10.1080/20479700.2019.1608636
- **PDF:** https://www.academia.edu/download/100388817/20479700.2019.160863620230329-1-ljmdkb.pdf
- **Cited by:** 5
- **Key findings:**
  - Healthcare-OH survey includes a Resilience dimension among organizational health measures
  - Validated via EFA and CFA
  - Inter-rater reliability calculated and improved after coder discussion

### 10. Cheng CKT, Chua JH, Cheng LJ, et al. Global prevalence of resilience in health care professionals: A systematic review, meta-analysis and meta-regression. Journal of Nursing Management. 2022.

- **URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jonm.13558
- **PDF:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1111/jonm.13558
- **Cited by:** 42
- **Key findings:**
  - Identifies main scales used: Brief Resilience Scale (BRS), Connor-Davidson Resilience Scale (CD-RISC), Wagnild and Young Resilience Scale (RS-14/RS-25), Brief Resilience Coping Scale (BRCS)
  - Prevalence of low resilience varies significantly by instrument used
  - Notes need for further psychometric validation among healthcare professionals
