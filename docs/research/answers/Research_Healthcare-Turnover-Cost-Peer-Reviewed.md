# Research: Peer-Reviewed Evidence for Healthcare Employee Turnover Costs

**Question:** What is the peer-reviewed evidence for the total cost of employee turnover in healthcare, including direct costs (recruitment, training) and indirect costs (knowledge loss, productivity decline, institutional memory erosion)? Specifically looking for studies that quantify turnover costs as a multiple of annual salary for healthcare IT, informatics, or analytics roles.

**Scope:** Paper1 (supports R9: replace @oracle2024 vendor reference with peer-reviewed source)

**Date searched:** 2026-03-28

**Source:** Google Scholar Labs (evaluated 243 top results, surfaced 10 relevant papers)

---

## Key Findings

1. **Healthcare turnover costs 3x annual salary** for nurses (Wynendaele et al. 2025, umbrella review, cited 17). This is a recent, peer-reviewed source that directly replaces the Oracle vendor white paper claim.
2. **Nursing turnover cost-to-salary ratios range from 0.31 to 1.3** (Halter et al. 2017, cited 602). Per-nurse costs: $10,098 to $88,000.
3. **IT employee replacement costs 1-7x annual salary** (Ghapanchi et al. 2013, cited 26). This is the broadest range and covers IT personnel specifically.
4. **IT worker replacement ~2x annual salary** (Maudgalya et al. 2006, cited 143). Specific to high-quality IT workers.
5. **Clinical informatics turnover costs $50,000-$230,000 per employee** (Hackney 2024). This is the only source specific to clinical informatics/CIPS departments. Total cost of 39 departures in one year: $1.95-$8.97 million.
6. **Healthcare staff turnover 0.75-2.0x annual salary** as rule of thumb for nurses (Buchan 2010, cited 149).
7. **Waldman et al. (2004) "The shocking cost of turnover in health care"** was NOT surfaced by this search, suggesting it may not be indexed in Scholar or may be behind a paywall. However, the results above provide multiple peer-reviewed alternatives.

## Recommended Replacement for @oracle2024

**Best replacement:** Wynendaele et al. 2025 (BMC Health Services Research, cited 17). It's recent (2025), peer-reviewed, open-access, and directly states "three times the salary of a nurse." Combined with Ghapanchi & Aurum 2011 (Journal of Systems and Software, cited 363) for the IT-specific claim that turnover costs include "loss of experienced individuals and their tacit knowledge."

**Alternative stronger pair:** Halter et al. 2017 (cited 602) + Maudgalya et al. 2006 (cited 143) for the healthcare nursing + IT cost ratio respectively.

For clinical informatics specifically, Hackney 2024 provides the most targeted data ($50K-$230K per informaticist departure).

## Sources

### 1. Wynendaele H, Clays E, Peeters E, et al. Understanding turnover in healthcare and welfare sectors of high-income countries: an umbrella review. BMC Health Services Research. 2025.

- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12913-025-12966-5
- **PDF:** https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1186/s12913-025-12966-5.pdf
- **Cited by:** 17
- **Key findings:**
  - **Cost of each individual staff turnover estimated at 3x salary of a nurse**
  - Organizational consequences include heavier workloads for remaining staff and higher hiring/training costs
  - Frequent team-level turnover results in **loss of important knowledge and experience**

### 2. Halter M, Boiko O, Pelone F, Beighton C, et al. The determinants and consequences of adult nursing staff turnover: a systematic review of systematic reviews. BMC Health Services Research. 2017;17:824.

- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12913-017-2707-0
- **PDF:** https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1186/s12913-017-2707-0.pdf
- **Cited by:** 602
- **Key findings:**
  - **Cost ratios of per-nurse turnover relative to annual salary: 0.31 to 1.3**
  - Per-nurse turnover costs: $10,098 to $88,000
  - Total organizational turnover cost: $0.55 million to $8.5 million
  - Studies from acute hospital settings in USA, Australasia, and Canada

### 3. Hackney A. Onboarding New Hires in the Clinical Informatics and Practice Support (CIPS) Department With the Tiered Skills Acquisition Model (TSAM): A Program Evaluation. ProQuest Dissertations. 2024.

- **URL:** https://search.proquest.com/openview/9a3a9ef301c01ff0129d07f685b8643f/1
- **Cited by:** new
- **Key findings:**
  - **Clinical informatics replacement costs: $50,000-$230,000 per employee** (hiring, training, orientation, lost productivity)
  - 39 employees leaving CIPS department in 2019 cost $1.95-$8.97 million total
  - Training program to reduce turnover estimated savings: $1-$4.8 million

### 4. Ghapanchi AH, Ghapanchi AR, et al. A systematic review on information technology personnel's turnover. Lecture Notes in Computer Science. 2013.

- **URL:** https://research-repository.griffith.edu.au/items/70427635-4ee5-552c-8e9c-6ec5d3775011
- **PDF:** https://research-repository.griffith.edu.au/bitstreams/1ab05f83-6612-5351-89ec-1100b8e2be71/download
- **Cited by:** 26
- **Key findings:**
  - **Replacing an IS/IT employee costs 1-7x annual salary**
  - Direct costs: recruiting and training expenses
  - Indirect costs: disruptions in organizational processes

### 5. Ghapanchi AH, Aurum A. Antecedents to IT personnel's intentions to leave: A systematic literature review. Journal of Systems and Software. 2011;84(2):238-249.

- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0164121210002645
- **Cited by:** 363
- **Key findings:**
  - Hiring costs, lost salaries, training investments, benefits, tax payments: **~$100,000 per senior IT employee who quits within 180 days**
  - Taxonomy of 70 distinct turnover determinants (individual, organizational, job-related, psychological, environmental)
  - Loss of experienced individuals and their **tacit knowledge** as key indirect cost

### 6. Cascio WF. The future world of work: Implications for human resource costing and accounting. Journal of Human Resource Costing & Accounting. 1998;3(2):9-19.

- **URL:** https://www.emerald.com/jhrca/article/3/2/9/227157
- **PDF:** https://www.emerald.com/jhrca/article-pdf/3/2/9/1525928/eb029045.pdf
- **Cited by:** 68
- **Key findings:**
  - Formula for calculating "uncompensated performance differential" between leaver and replacement
  - Increasing cost of turnover for technical and managerial talent in IT
  - Costs extend beyond traditional separation, replacement, and training expenses

### 7. Bae SH. Noneconomic and economic impacts of nurse turnover in hospitals: A systematic review. International Nursing Review. 2022;69(3):405-420.

- **URL:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/inr.12769
- **PDF:** https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1111/inr.12769
- **Cited by:** 350
- **Key findings:**
  - Per-nurse turnover costs: $62,100-$67,100 (2005), $82,000-$88,000 (2008, inflation-adjusted)
  - Additional costs for hiring ($1,635) and training ($15,825) plus reduced new employee effectiveness
  - Costs categorized as pre-hire/post-hire or direct/indirect; sometimes presented as ratio to salary

### 8. Maudgalya T, Wallace S, Daraiseh N. Workplace stress factors and 'burnout' among information technology professionals: A systematic review. Theoretical Issues in Ergonomics Science. 2006;7(3):285-297.

- **URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/14639220500090638
- **Cited by:** 143
- **Key findings:**
  - **Replacing a high-quality IT worker costs approximately 2x annual salary**
  - IT profession has high turnover rates and low satisfaction compared to other service sectors
  - Stress factors (long hours, tight deadlines, under-staffing) linked to burnout and turnover

### 9. Buchan J. Reviewing the benefits of health workforce stability. Human Resources for Health. 2010;8:29.

- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/1478-4491-8-29
- **PDF:** https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1186/1478-4491-8-29.pdf
- **Cited by:** 149
- **Key findings:**
  - Total turnover costs for US hospital system of 5,000 employees: $17-$29 million
  - **Rule of thumb: turnover cost is 0.75-2.0x annual salary for staff nurse**
  - Individual nurse turnover cost: $21,500-$31,500

### 10. Jones CB. Revisiting nurse turnover costs: Adjusting for inflation. JONA: The Journal of Nursing Administration. 2008;38(1):11-18.

- **URL:** https://journals.lww.com/jonajournal/fulltext/2008/01000/Nurse_Staffing_and_Healthcare_Outcomes__A.00004.aspx
- **PDF:** https://journals.lww.com/jonajournal/_layouts/15/oaks.journals/downloadpdf.aspx?an=00005110-200801000-00004
- **Cited by:** 541
- **Key findings:**
  - Nurse turnover costs: ~$22,000 to >$64,000 per nurse
  - Variability depends on whether indirect costs (productivity losses) are captured
  - Cost categories: prehire (advertising, recruiting, vacancy, hiring) and posthire (orientation, training, productivity losses, termination)
