# Research Question: What quantitative efficiency metrics (time savings, task completion rates) have been reported for low-code or conversational AI in healthcare analytics?

**Scope:** Paper1

**Date:** 2025-12-20
**Results Found:** 10 relevant papers
**Sorted By:** Relevance
**Search Duration:** ~2 minutes
**Search Queries Used:**
- "What quantitative efficiency metrics (time savings, task completion rates) have been reported for low-code or conversational AI in healthcare analytics?"

---

## Summary of Findings

This search found 10 highly relevant papers (2020-2025) providing substantial quantitative evidence for efficiency metrics in both conversational AI and low-code platforms applied to healthcare. Key findings include: low-code development achieving 55-94.63% time/effort reductions, conversational AI achieving 83-92.5% task completion rates, and real-world implementations showing 27-67% workflow efficiency improvements. The literature comprehensively addresses this research question with peer-reviewed empirical data.

---

## 1. The effectiveness of artificial intelligence conversational agents in health care: systematic review

**Authors:** M Milne-Ives, C De Cock, E Lim, MH Shehadeh, et al.
**Publication:** Journal of Medical Internet Research (JMIR), 2020
**Citations:** 557 (139.25/year)
**Link:** https://www.jmir.org/2020/10/e20346/
**PDF:** https://www.jmir.org/2020/10/e20346/PDF

**Abstract/Summary:**
Systematic review assessing effectiveness and usability of conversational agents in healthcare for tasks like triage, screening, and treatment support. Notes that few studies quantify efficiency claims objectively.

**Methodology/Approach:** Systematic Review

**Key Points:**
- Conversational agents claimed to reduce burden on healthcare resources, save providers' time, and lower costs
- Very few studies quantify these claims or measure outcomes objectively
- Identifies need for future cost-effectiveness data
- Reviews potential for efficiency metrics like time savings or task completion rates

**Relationship to Other Papers:** Foundational systematic review cited by many subsequent studies; establishes the research gap that later papers address.

**Methodology/Approach:** Framework Development, Metrics Analysis

**Key Points:**

- Encompasses language processing, clinical task impact, and conversation effectiveness
- Highlights workload burden mitigation for healthcare providers
- Implies efficiency and time savings as key outcome measures

**Relationship to Other Papers:** Provides the theoretical metrics framework that empirical studies (like Rollwage et al.) apply in practice.

## 4. A systematic review on healthcare artificial intelligent conversational agents for chronic conditions

**Authors:** A Bin Sawad, B Narayan, A Alnefaie, A Maqbool, et al.
**Publication:** Sensors (MDPI), 2022
**Citations:** 103 (34.33/year)
**Link:** https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/22/7/2625
**PDF:** Not available

**Abstract/Summary:**
Systematic review of AI conversational agents for chronic disease management, reporting specific task completion and accuracy metrics.

**Methodology/Approach:** Systematic Review

**Key Points:**
- Task completion rate: **84.8%** (Cohen's D, k = 0.848) for glaucoma/diabetic conditions
- Conversational agent accuracy: **89%**
- Depression chatbot: **99.2% accuracy**, **99.7% specificity**
- Demonstrates efficiency in daily education and support for chronic disease patients

**Relationship to Other Papers:** Provides disease-specific metrics that complement the broader systematic reviews by Milne-Ives and Li et al.

## 6. Traditional vs. low-code development: comparing needed effort and system complexity in the NexusBRaNT experiment

**Publication:** 2023 IEEE 25th Conference on Business Informatics (CBI), 2023
**Citations:** 22 (11/year)
**Link:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10186753
**PDF:** Not available

**Abstract/Summary:**
Controlled experiment comparing effort required for low-code vs traditional development for a healthcare system targeting health professionals in cognitive rehabilitation.

**Methodology/Approach:** Controlled Experiment

**Key Points:**

- **94.63% reduction** in needed effort

## Key Themes and Observations

1. **Low-Code Development Efficiency:** Multiple studies report 50-94.63% reductions in development time/effort when using low-code platforms for healthcare applications (Aveiro: 94.63%, Seitzhan: 55%, Mogili: 67%).

2. **Conversational AI Task Completion:** Completion rates for healthcare conversational agents consistently range from 83-92.5% across multiple systematic reviews and empirical studies.

3. **Real-World Validation:** Large-scale real-world studies (Rollwage: 64,862 patients; Mogili: multinational deployment) validate efficiency gains seen in controlled experiments.

4. **Physician Time Savings:** Multiple papers identify time savings for clinicians through automated triage, patient inquiry handling, and assessment facilitation.

5. **Healthcare-Specific Metrics:** Studies increasingly provide healthcare-contextualized metrics (clinical documentation efficiency, appointment adherence, mental health assessment time).

## Relevance to Three-Pillar Framework

1. **Analytics maturity:** Low-code platforms (55-94% efficiency gains) can accelerate healthcare organizations' progression through analytics maturity stages by reducing development barriers. The 67% reduction in development timelines directly addresses the resource constraints limiting analytics adoption.

2. **Workforce turnover:** Conversational AI (83-92.5% task completion) can preserve institutional knowledge by encoding analyst expertise into queryable systems. The time savings for physicians (handling routine inquiries) reduces burden on knowledge workers.

3. **Technical barriers:** Query interpretation accuracy of 92.5% for biomedical queries demonstrates that NL2SQL-style interfaces can effectively bridge the gap between clinical users and complex data systems. Real-time analysis completion (<1 minute) validates practical utility.

---

## Gaps Identified

1. **Healthcare analytics-specific NL2SQL metrics:** Most studies focus on patient-facing conversational AI rather than analyst-facing query interfaces
2. **Longitudinal efficiency tracking:** Few studies measure sustained efficiency gains over time
3. **Cost-effectiveness analysis:** Quantitative ROI data remains limited despite efficiency metrics
4. **Comparison across healthcare domains:** Limited cross-specialty comparisons of efficiency gains
