## 1. Right Panel ("The Compounding Crisis") -- Text Correction

**Current text**: "NL2SQL improving but, sufficiently inaccurate"

**Problem**: This reverses the meaning of the paper's finding. The manuscript states that current models are "not yet sufficiently **accurate** for unsupervised use" (Abstract, Results; Section 3.3, citing Ziletti & D'Ambrosi 2024). "Sufficiently inaccurate" implies the inaccuracy is adequate or expected, which is the opposite of the paper's cautionary claim. The comma placement is also non-standard.

**Requested change**: Replace with one of the following:

- "NL2SQL improving but not yet accurate enough for unsupervised use"
- "NL2SQL improving; accuracy gaps remain for clinical use"

---

## 2. Left Panel ("Interacting organizational and technical challenges...") -- Causal Direction

**Current layout**: The left panel shows a linear top-to-bottom cascade:

> Low analytics maturity --> High leadership turnover --> Skill shortage --> Institutional memory loss

**Problem**: The manuscript defines a specific causal chain (Introduction, Section 1): Analytics Maturity is the **observable symptom** (Pillar 1), Workforce Instability is the **active driver** (Pillar 2), and Technical Barriers are the **root mechanism** (Pillar 3). The current cascade implies that low maturity *causes* turnover, but the paper argues the reverse: turnover drives low maturity, and technical barriers perpetuate the cycle. The paper's core insight is that these challenges are **cyclical and self-reinforcing**, not a linear chain.

**Requested change**: Revise the left panel to show a cyclical or circular relationship among the three challenges rather than a one-directional cascade. If a cycle is not feasible within the current layout, reversing the arrow direction (institutional memory loss --> low analytics maturity, i.e., bottom-to-top) and adding a return arrow from the hospital icon back to the top would convey the compounding loop.

## 4. Center or Bottom Panel -- Add the Validated Query Cycle

**Current state**: The center panel shows the three-pillar framework structure. The bottom banner references "technological interventions, including conversational AI platforms" but does not depict the paper's proposed mechanism.

**Problem**: The manuscript's third contribution (Section 1.5, Figures 1-2) is the **Validated Query Cycle**, a six-step process (Query --> Generation --> Validation --> Storage --> Retrieval --> Persistence) that converts tacit knowledge into durable organizational memory. This is the paper's most actionable and differentiating element -- the mechanism that breaks the compounding crisis. Its omission reduces the graphic to "here are three problems" without showing "here is a mechanism to address them."

**Requested change**: Add a simplified representation of the Validated Query Cycle, either as a small circular diagram in the center panel (below the pillar building) or as a new element between the center and right panels. A minimal version could show the six steps as icons in a circle with a label such as "Validated Query Cycle: captures expertise before it is lost."

If space is constrained, an alternative would be to revise the bottom banner to read: "The three-pillar framework reveals a compounding crisis and motivates the Validated Query Cycle -- a six-step mechanism that captures analyst expertise as durable organizational memory, guiding future research on conversational AI platforms."
