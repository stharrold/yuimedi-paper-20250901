Reviewer Q:
General comments
=============
Thank you for addressing the reviewers' comments. The current draft has sufficient potential for publication in IJMR.
My only recommendation is that the proposed model has strong potential to be further expanded and empirically evaluated. Two particularly promising directions for future work are:
1. Empirical assessment of the human-in-the-loop component. Specifically, the authors could investigate whether incorporating a human expert into the validation process leads to a measurable improvement in the accuracy and quality of the generated knowledge base compared to a fully automated approach.
2. Comparative evaluation against existing models. The proposed framework could be compared with established approaches in a controlled or simulated setting to assess differences in the quality, completeness, and reliability of the generated knowledge base when responding to the same set of inquiries.
While I do not consider these analyses essential for the current manuscript, I believe they represent valuable avenues for future research that would further strengthen and validate the proposed framework.
